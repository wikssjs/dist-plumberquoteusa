Permits & regulations in Pennsylvania

In Pennsylvania—including Pequea—plumbing work must be performed by, or under the direct supervision of, a licensed Master Plumber or Journeyman Plumber. Licenses are issued through the PA Department of Labor & Industry; verify status and classification at dli.pa.gov (PA Plumbing Board resources are linked there). For major work, Pennsylvania and local township or municipal code requirements typically mandate permits, inspections, and compliant installation practices.

Permits are commonly required for water heater installation, sewer line work, new fixture installations, and pipe replacement beyond minor repairs (especially when walls are opened or the work changes plumbing routing). Repairs like fixing a leaking faucet cartridge or replacing a toilet flapper typically do not require a permit.

Lead service lines are a special concern. Under PA Act 2021-41, water utilities replace lead service lines, but the homeowner-side lateral from the street to the home may still contain lead in pre-1986 housing. Ask your plumber to recommend testing/documentation of service line material and to coordinate appropriately.

Don’t skip insurance verification. Confirm workers’ compensation coverage using wcais.pa.gov. Also request a certificate of insurance that lists your property address. Homeowners can face significant liability if uninsured workers are injured on their property.

How to verify step-by-step in Pequea: (1) look up the license at dli.pa.gov, (2) request a certificate of insurance with your address on it, (3) verify workers’ comp status through wcais.pa.gov, and (4) confirm the plumber will pull permits and schedule inspections for the specific scope you’re authorizing.
